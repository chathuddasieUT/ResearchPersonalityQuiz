About this test

Understanding researcher personalities

This test reflects how you approach research across five areas: planning, reading, experimentation, publication, and specialization.

Both sides of each dimension are valuable. Research thrives on different styles, from careful planning to exploration, from deep focus to broad scanning.

Most people lean one way, but strong researchers adapt when needed. Being flexible in how you work is a real advantage.

This also matters when choosing a lab or mentor. Success often depends on how well your working style fits the environment.

Use this as a guide, not a rule. What matters most is the science. If the work excites you, pursue it, while staying aware of how you and your environment work together.
